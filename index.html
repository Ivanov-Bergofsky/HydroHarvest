Why Harvest Rainwater?

Rainwater harvesting is the simple process of collecting and storing rainwater that falls onto roofs, terraces, or open grounds. It reduces reliance on municipal water supply, replenishes groundwater levels, and provides a free, sustainable water source.

Harvesting Techniques

Rooftop Harvesting

Collecting rain from roof surfaces and directing it to storage tanks or recharge pits.

Surface Runoff

Harvesting rainwater flowing along the ground into reservoirs or tanks.

Recharge Pits

Pits filled with boulders and gravel to increase groundwater recharge.

Percolation Tanks

Artificially created surface water bodies submerged in highly permeable land.

Recharge Wells

Using existing dug wells to recharge the deep aquifers directly.

Rain Barrels

Simple containers to catch runoff from downspouts for garden use.

Essential Products

First Flush Diverter

Flushes off the first spell of rain which carries dirt and dust from the roof, ensuring cleaner water enters the tank.

Leaf Eaters / Gutters

PVC or GI gutters with mesh guards to prevent leaves and debris from clogging the conveyance system.

Filtration Units

Sand, carbon, or fabric filters that remove fine sediments and impurities before storage.
